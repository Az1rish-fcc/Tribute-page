Dietrich Bonhoeffer

Lutheran Pastor and Nazi Resister

A sketch of Dietrich Bonhoeffer.

    Here is a timeline of Bonhoeffer's life:
  • 4 Feb 1906 - Born in Breslau, Prussia (now Wroclaw, Poland).
  • 1923 - Begins theological studies at Tubingen University.
  • 1930 - Bonhoeffer travels to New York to begin teaching fellowship at Union Theological Seminary.
  • 1931 - He returns to Germany.
  • 1933 - Hitler is installed as Chancellor. Two days later Bonhoeffer delivers a radio address attacking Hitler. He is cut off the air.
  • 1933 - The first ever national Church of Germany is established. Also, legislation is passed banning Jews from public employment.
  • 1934 - Bonhoeffer and others organize the Confessing Church at Barmen, Germany. They adopt a declaration insisting that Christ, not the Fuhrer, is the head of the church. Bonhoeffer leaves for England to head a church for Germans there.
  • 2 Aug 1934 - German President Paul von Hindenburg dies. Hitler is proclaimed both Chancellor and President.
  • 1935 - Bonhoeffer returns from England to direct the seminary for the Confessing Church in Finkenwalde, Germany. By the end of the year all training there is declared invalid and all participants liable to arrest.
  • Sep 1935 - The Nuremberg Laws are passed, cancelling citizenship for German Jews.
  • Jul 1936 - Leader of the Confessing Church, Martin Niemoller, is arrested.
  • Aug 1936 - Bonhoeffer's authorization to teach at Berlin University is withdrawn.
  • Sep 1937 - The serminary at Finkenwalde is closed by the Gestapo.
  • Nov 1937 - 27 pastors and former Finkenwalde students are arrested.
  • 1940 - Bonhoeffer is forbidden to speak in public and is required to report regularly to the police.
  • 1941 - Bonhoeffer is forbidden to print or to publish.
  • Jan 1943 - Bonhoeffer proposes and becomes engaged to Maria von Wedemeyer.
  • 5 April 1943 - He is arrested and incarcerated at Tegel Prison, Berlin.
  • Oct 1944 - Bonhoeffer is moved to the Gestapo prison at Prinz-Albrecht-Strasse, Berlin.
  • 19 Dec 1944 - Bonhoeffer writes his final letter to Maria von Wedemeyer.
  • 3 Apr 1945 - Bonhoeffer is moved to Regensberg.
  • 8 Apr 1945 - He is moved to Flossenberg concentration camp in the middle of the night.
  • 9 Apr 1945 - Bonhoeffer and other key members of the resistance are executed at Flossenberg concentration camp.
  • 30 Apr 1945 - Adolf Hitler commits suicide in his Berlin bunker.
  • 2 May 1945 - Berlin falls.
  • 7 May 1945 - German forces make an unconditional surrender.

Dietrich Bonhoeffer was an incredibly courageous man who helped many Jews escape Nazi Germany during World War II. If you are interested in learning more about him, please visit this Wikipedia page.
